Our Research

Exploring heritage conservation, sustainable materials, and digital innovation.

Research Themes

Our cluster integrates multidisciplinary approaches to heritage conservation, combining structural engineering, digital technologies, and human-centered design. We promote research that advances the documentation, repair, and sustainable management of built cultural heritage.

Human-Centric Digital Twins

Developing FAIR-aligned frameworks for digital documentation, management, and presentation of built cultural heritage assets.

Learn more

Structural Conservation

Advancing methods for the analysis, repair, and rehabilitation of traditional and historic structures through sustainable engineering practices.

Learn more

Regenerative Design

Exploring regenerative principles and materials, such as earthen and stone structures, for a circular and zero-carbon built environment.

Learn more

Ongoing Projects

Scaling Up Structural Stone

Investigating strategies to increase the use of locally sourced stone for sustainable construction, aligning with zero-carbon goals.

Supervisors: Dr. Alejandro Jiménez Rios, Dr. Will Hawkins, Prof. Antony Darby

Recyclability of Earthen Materials

Assessing and quantifying the recyclability of earthen construction materials to inform regenerative conservation and adaptive reuse.

Supervisors: Dr. Alejandro Jiménez Rios, Dr. Daniel Maskell

Regenerative Post-Conflict Reconstruction

Integrating debris re-use, traditional techniques, and community-centred approaches to heritage reconstruction in post-conflict settings.

Supervisors: Dr. Alejandro Jiménez Rios, Dr. Taghried Abdelmagid, Dr. Abobakr Elwakeel

Reeco-Soil Project

Investigating ecologically active soil structures as part of regenerative retrofitting strategies aligned with RENEW's principles.

Lead Researcher: Dr. Alejandro Jiménez Rios
